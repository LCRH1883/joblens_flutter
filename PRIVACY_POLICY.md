# Privacy Policy for Joblens

Last updated: April 5, 2026

Intagri Technologies LLC, a Delaware, USA company ("Intagri," "Joblens," "we," "us," or "our"), provides the Joblens mobile application and related services.

This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, store, and protect information when you use the Joblens app on iOS or Android and related backend services. It is intended to support distribution through the Apple App Store and Google Play and to provide notice to users in North America, Europe, and other regions where Joblens is offered.

If you have privacy questions or want to exercise your rights, contact us at: Privacy@joblens.xyz

## 1. Scope

This Privacy Policy applies to:

- the Joblens iOS and Android applications;
- the Joblens backend and sync services;
- Joblens account creation, sign-in, password recovery, provider connection, media sync, and related support interactions.

This Privacy Policy does not govern:

- the privacy practices of Apple, Google, Supabase, or the cloud storage providers you choose to connect, except to the extent we control how those services are used within Joblens; or
- websites, applications, or services operated by third parties and linked from Joblens.

## 2. What Joblens Does

Joblens is a photo capture, organization, and sync app. Users can:

- create a Joblens account;
- capture photos in the app or import photos from their device library;
- organize photos into projects;
- create project notes;
- connect a cloud storage provider account they already own, such as Google Drive, OneDrive, Dropbox, Box, or Nextcloud; and
- sync supported content between their devices and their selected cloud storage.

Provider-owned cloud storage is intended to be the canonical home for synced media. Joblens uses Supabase for app authentication and backend coordination, and the app also stores a local device cache for usability and offline access.

## 3. Information We Collect

The information we collect depends on how you use Joblens.

### A. Account and identity information

We may collect:

- your email address;
- your Joblens authentication records and session information;
- account profile information you provide, such as display name, if enabled or provided.

### B. Content you create, upload, or sync

We may collect or process:

- photos you capture or import into Joblens;
- photo metadata such as file name, file type, file size, checksum/hash, timestamps, project association, and sync status;
- project information such as project name, optional description, and optional start date;
- project notes you create in the app;
- derived media needed for app functionality, such as thumbnails or previews;
- records of whether content exists locally, in cloud storage, or both.

At the time of this policy update, synced media and project metadata are core features. Project notes may be stored locally on your device and may not yet be available as a fully cloud-synced feature in all versions of Joblens.

### C. Cloud provider connection information

When you connect a cloud provider, we may collect or process:

- the provider you selected;
- the connected account identifier and display name returned by the provider;
- authorization tokens, refresh tokens, or provider credentials needed to maintain the connection;
- sync target details such as remote folder paths, remote file identifiers, and remote metadata;
- for Nextcloud, server URL, username, and app password that you provide to establish the connection.

Provider secrets are intended to be stored on the backend in encrypted form, not as the source of truth on the mobile device.

### D. Device, app, and sync information

We may collect or process:

- device identifiers generated by Joblens for sync;
- device platform and device name;
- app version;
- timestamps such as last seen, last sync, and sync event times;
- sync job records, conflict records, retry state, and deletion tombstones;
- diagnostic information related to sync operations, such as asset IDs, project IDs, provider types, errors, and operation timestamps.

### E. Support and communications

If you contact us or share logs with us, we may collect:

- your email address and correspondence;
- support details you choose to send;
- exported sync logs and attachments you choose to share.

### F. Technical and network information

Like most online services, our backend, reverse proxies, hosting, and infrastructure providers may process limited technical data such as:

- IP address;
- request timestamps;
- device or browser user-agent data;
- error and security logs.

We use this information to operate, secure, and troubleshoot the service.

## 4. Information We Access Through Device Permissions

Joblens may request the following device permissions:

- Camera: to capture photos in the app.
- Photo library/media access: to let you browse and import photos from your device.
- Internet/network access: to authenticate, connect providers, sync data, and load provider-hosted media through Joblens backend services.

Joblens does not currently request location, contacts, microphone, call log, SMS, or advertising identifier access for core app functionality.

On some devices, you may also choose an import mode that removes imported photos from phone storage after they are added to Joblens. That is a user-controlled feature.

## 5. How We Use Information

We use information to:

- create and manage your Joblens account;
- authenticate you and maintain sign-in sessions;
- let you capture, import, organize, tag, and view photos;
- create and manage projects and project notes;
- connect and maintain your chosen cloud provider account;
- sync photos, media metadata, and project structure across your devices and connected cloud storage where those features are available;
- generate thumbnails, previews, and download URLs needed for app functionality;
- troubleshoot sync failures, repair inconsistencies, and resolve conflicts;
- provide support and respond to requests;
- protect the security and integrity of the app, backend, and connected accounts;
- comply with legal obligations and enforce our terms.

We do not use your photos or project data to serve third-party advertising inside Joblens.

## 7. No Sale of Personal Information

Joblens is not designed to sell personal information or personal and sensitive user data for monetary consideration. We also do not use third-party advertising SDKs in the current app architecture.

If this changes in the future, we will update this Privacy Policy and any required in-app or store disclosures.

## 8. Data Retention

We retain information for as long as reasonably necessary for the purposes described above, including to provide the service, maintain sync integrity, comply with law, resolve disputes, and enforce agreements.

Examples:

- Account and backend records: generally while your account remains active, plus a reasonable period for security, legal, backup, and operational needs.
- Connected provider records: until you disconnect the provider, delete your account, or we otherwise no longer need the connection data.
- Local device cache: remains on your device until deleted by you, cleared by the app, or removed when the app is uninstalled.
- Sync logs stored locally in the app: remain until you clear them or remove the app.
- Support materials you send us: retained as needed to resolve the issue and maintain appropriate records.

If you request account deletion from within the app, we intend to delete your Joblens account and backend data associated with that account, subject to any legitimate retention needs. However, deleting a Joblens account does not automatically delete files, folders, or other content stored in your own connected cloud provider account. You are responsible for managing or deleting those items in the provider account if desired.

## 9. Security

We use administrative, technical, and organizational measures intended to protect personal data. These may include access controls, encryption in transit, encryption of sensitive backend secrets, authentication controls, and logging.

No method of transmission or storage is completely secure. We therefore cannot guarantee absolute security.
